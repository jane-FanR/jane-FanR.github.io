About Us

Our history starts back in 1968 as a small family business, with humble origins when Mr. O'Neill, his wife Elizabeth “Betty” and his 3 sons ,decided to shape their dreams in a tangible reality.
Betty was known by their neighbours in a small village in Gorey, co.Wexford as a great cook, her Guiness Pie was simply decadent, with this “mom's touch”. They would sell it regularly to the few inhabitants in Gorey back then but the word spread fast until the point the O'Neills were famous in the whole county. Mr. O'Neill and his sons were in charge of the deliveries but the demand rose to the point they were afraid of not attending to and lose customers - they could not afford that.
Then, only 3 years later, in 1971, they opened their first pub - Bettys. It was an immediate success but the family started to face demand issues again. However, that humble family from Wexford had bigger dreams and Mr. O'Neill decided to expand his wife's talent further away but now, in Dublin. In 1975, John and Michael O'Neill moved to the capital Dublin in order to keep the Bettys Pie known throughout Ireland. However, the boys chose a restaurant focused business over pubs as the segment was very competitive - and it worked. For years, the name O'Neill was associated with traditional and top-class Irish food until recently when the family decided to get a lift on this contemporaneous wave when their pioneer sense guided them once again to a new business - a steakhouse. Keeping quality since that small farm in Gorey, prioritising the customer's experience, a “Mum's Love” as our motto says, rendered our stake house the ultimate recognition known in gastronomy, amongst many others, where you can find here.

The "Mum's Touch", or "Mum's Love".
